BioMed Central today announced (“BioMed Central Blog,” 2012) that the Howard Hughes Medical Institute (“Howard Hughes Medical Institute (HHMI) Biomedical Research & Science Education,” 2012) will become a BioMed Central member (read the HHMI announcement here (n.d.-a)). HHMI will pay all article processing charges for HHMI investigators in BioMed Central journals starting September 1.

This announcement comes two months after HHMI started to require (“HHMI News,” 2012b) that all HHMI-supported research has to become publicly available 6 months after publication and at a time when Yale University drops (“Yale University Drops Biomed Central Membership - Gobbledygook - Martin Fenner’s Blog on Nature Network,” 2008) their BioMed Central membership.

In March (“HHMI News,” 2012a) HHMI announced that all research published in Elsevier journals will be publicly available 6 months after publication, again starting September 1.

Congratulations HHMI.
